About Me

I have been programming on and off almost my entire life. When I was 13, my dad brought home a computer from work, but it had no storage drive. It only had DOS. I spent many hours learning Basic to try and create something. Unfortunately, anything I made I would lose as soon as the computer was shut down. I still spent hours on that computer typing away.

After that, my life went down a different path as I graduated high school and then went on to spend 6 years in the U.S. Navy as an electrical technician on fast attack submarines operating and maintaining the nuclear power plant. I left the military after my initial enlistment and worked for a few years as a technician before returning to college for a degree in Computer Information Systems.

After completing my B.S., I decided to learn more about business and planning on combining my information knowledge with business knowledge to target a career in digital marketing. I returned to school and completed an M.B.A. focused on marketing and information management.

I worked for a while in an operations role before heading out on my own and working as a digital marketing consultant and helped found the United States Veterans Chamber of Commerce. During this time, I began reflecting over my career and I realized my favorite part of every job I held was writing or troubleshooting code. I was always most engaged when working on any program from ladder logic in PLCs, to building marketing segmentation algorithms, or working on my hobby and building a world using Unity or Unreal.

I am now continuing down the path that I started early in my life. I realize that I had to go back to the beginning to find the path again, but I found it and the am ready for the journey this time.

Ecommerce Decision Prediction Model

In this project, I assumed the role of a marketing data scientist for an ecommerce company. With the goal of increasing website conversions, I examined user interaction with the website and then made a prediction on whether the user was likely to make a purchase or not. From here I made recommendations based on the prediction to increase conversion rate. Logistic regression was used to make the predictions.

Indie Game Launch Prediction

In this project, I assumed the role of a marketing data scientist for an indie game company. I created a model to help drive sales of a newly launched game by creating a targeted prediction of user interaction to reach 20k sales. This project is focused on determining where the game engagement level should be within 1 to 2 years from launch. I used data from Steam and also scraped data to find top game companies for a point of reference. Linear Regression was used to build the prediction model.

LEGO Set Value Prediction

This project took a look at an alternative form of investment outside the typical markets (stocks, property, bitcoin) and looked at LEGO sets as an investment. I looked at how the value of LEGO sets have increased since they were first launched and considered multiple properties of the set to predict whether or not the set would be a good investment. I used the S&P 500 as a benchmark for good investment. Random Forest Classifier was used to create the model.
